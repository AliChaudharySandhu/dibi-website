Gender Equality, Disability, and Social Inclusion (GEDSI)

Embedding equity in transport planning and electrification pathways.

Principles

  • Ensure access and safety for women, people with disabilities, and vulnerable groups.
  • Co-design with local communities and operators to reflect lived experience.
  • Use evidence-based, intersectional analysis for decisions and investment.
  • Measure benefits distribution and reduce barriers to participation.

Methods

  • Inclusive surveys and focus groups at terminals and along routes.
  • Accessibility audits of depots, buses, stops, and information systems.
  • Disaggregated data dashboards (sex, age, disability) to track outcomes.
  • Policy and design recommendations with implementation guidance.

Expected Outcomes

  • More equitable service design and depot operations.
  • Safer, more comfortable public transport environments.
  • Increased participation in planning and feedback loops.
