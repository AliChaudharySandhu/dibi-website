About the DIBI Project

Decarbonisation Pathways for Indonesia’s Buses Infrastructure

Overview

DIBI is a KONEKSI-supported initiative co-designed by Australian and Indonesian partners to help cities plan and scale electric bus adoption. The project blends routing and energy estimation, renewable energy integration, and ecosystem-based planning with a strong emphasis on social inclusion (GEDSI).

Objectives

  • Develop practical pathways for bus fleet electrification and depot readiness.
  • Assess energy efficiency, thermal/noise comfort, and pollution reduction at depots.
  • Integrate solar PV and battery storage within charging strategies.
  • Evaluate community impact using GEDSI criteria to ensure equitable outcomes.

Who we work with

A collaboration across government, academia, industry, and civil society.

  • • Indonesian transport authorities (e.g., Surakarta)
  • • Universities (UNS, UMN, UC)
  • • Industry partners (NEVCE, EV Energi, ITP Renewables)
  • • Research and NGOs (ICE, ICLEI, BRIN)
Scope: This public website shares project context, updates, and outputs to support knowledge exchange and policy dialogue.

Timeframe

2024–2025 (Pilot and dissemination)

Locations

Surakarta, Jakarta, ACT & Sydney

Focus Areas

Energy planning, renewables, air quality, GEDSI
